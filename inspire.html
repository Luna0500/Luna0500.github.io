Kizzmekia "Kizzy" Shanta Corbett

Kizzmekia Corbett is an American viral immunologist whose research on Covid lead to the development of the Covid-19 vaccines.

The Covid vaccine works by building a non-functional part of the virus inside your body, specifically the spikes you can see on the outside. However, if you try to build the spikes without them being attached to a virus body, their structure collapses.

Kizzmekia and other researchers, including folks at Mclellen Labs figured out in 2017 that if you put a double Proline substitution in the right place in the mRNA, it acts like a "splint", preventing the structure from collapsing.

She gives speeches often, attempting to address vaccine hesitancy and misinformation surrounding vaccines.
